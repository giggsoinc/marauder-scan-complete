PatronAI

AI Security Dashboard — monitor, investigate, and respond to AI usage across your organisation in real time.

Version 1.0.0 Audience Executives, Managers, Support, Admins Deployed on EC2 · Browser-based

Overview

PatronAI is a browser-based security dashboard that gives your organisation complete visibility into AI tool usage — who is using which AI services, what data is being sent, and where the risks are concentrated.

Key capabilities at a glance:

  • AI Landscape — geographic and flow visualisations of AI provider traffic.
  • Risk Heatmap — identify which user or device combinations carry the highest risk score.
  • Data Exposure — track sensitive data categories reaching AI endpoints.
  • User Intelligence — per-user asset mind maps, findings, and risk summaries.
  • Reports — seven on-demand PDF reports covering executives through individual users.
  • Ask AI — a conversational interface backed by a local LLM (no data leaves your EC2 instance).
  • Scanning Agents — lightweight agents deployed to endpoints that feed data back to the dashboard.
Note — Deployment

PatronAI runs entirely on your EC2 instance. All data, including the AI chat LLM, stays inside your infrastructure. Nothing is sent to a third-party cloud by default.

Roles & Access

Every user account has one of four roles. What you see in the dashboard depends on your role.

Note — Admin is a flag, not a separate role

The admin flag can be added on top of any role. An admin-flagged user gains the Settings tab and full access to all views regardless of their base role. Admins manage users, branding, scanning rules, and agent deployment.

Logging In

PatronAI is accessed through a standard web browser. No software installation is required on your workstation.

  1. Open your browser and navigate to the URL provided by your admin (e.g. http://<your-ec2-ip>:8501).
  2. Enter your email address and the password you received in your welcome email.
  3. The dashboard loads directly into the view appropriate for your role.
Tip — First-time login

You will receive a welcome email from your admin when your account is created. Use the credentials in that email. Contact your admin if you did not receive it or need a password reset.

The Exec View is the top-level summary dashboard. It is designed for quick situational awareness — leadership can understand the organisation's AI risk posture in under a minute.

KPI Tiles

Five large metric tiles run across the top of the Exec View. Each tile shows a live count calculated over the currently selected time window.

TileWhat it countsDrill-down shows

Clicking a KPI tile

Click any KPI tile to open an inline drill-down panel directly below the tiles. The panel shows the matching events in a sortable table. Click the tile again to collapse the panel.

Tip — Sorting the drill-down

Click any column header in the drill-down table to sort ascending or descending. Click the user's email to jump to their full User Detail page.

Tabs: AI Landscape, Risk Heatmap, Data Exposure

Below the KPI tiles, three tabs provide deeper analytical views.

AI Landscape

Contains two visualisations side by side:

  • Choropleth map — world map shaded by volume of AI provider traffic per country. Darker shading means more traffic. Hover a country to see provider count and request volume.
  • Sankey diagram — flow chart from Users → AI Categories → AI Providers. Width of each band is proportional to traffic volume. Useful for seeing which teams drive which providers.
[Screenshot: AI Landscape tab — choropleth map on left, Sankey diagram on right]

Risk Heatmap

A matrix heatmap where rows are users (or user groups) and columns are risk categories. Each cell is colour-coded from green (low) through amber to red (critical). Hovering a cell shows the individual score and contributing factors. Use this view to identify which users are consistently appearing in high-risk cells.

[Screenshot: Risk Heatmap tab — matrix with colour gradient cells]

Data Exposure

Tracks sensitive data categories (PII, financial, credentials, source code, etc.) that have been detected in AI traffic. Shows a bar chart of category volume and a timeline of exposure events. Use this tab to answer "is sensitive data being sent to AI providers?"

[Screenshot: Data Exposure tab — bar chart of categories + timeline below]

Manager View manager support admin

The Manager View provides operational depth. It is designed for managers and analysts who need to investigate specific users, assets, risks, and pipeline activity.

[Screenshot: Manager View — tab bar showing Inventory, Risks, Logs, Pipeline, AI Inventory]

Manager Tabs

Inventory

A table of all monitored users and devices. Each row shows user email, device count, active AI providers, last seen timestamp, and overall risk score. Click a column header to sort. Click a user's email to open their User Detail page.

[Screenshot: Inventory tab — user/device table with sortable columns]

Risks

A filterable list of all risk events. Use the filter dropdowns to narrow by severity, category, date range, or user. Each row expands to show the full finding detail including raw evidence and recommended remediation action.

Tip — Exporting risks

Use the Reports section to generate a formatted PDF of risk data. The Risks tab is for live investigation; Reports is for sharing with stakeholders.

[Screenshot: Risks tab — filterable findings list with expanded row]

Logs

Raw event log — every network event captured by scanning agents, sorted newest first. Useful for forensic investigation. Filter by user, provider, or date range. Each row shows: timestamp, user, device, destination, bytes, provider classification, and findings flags.

[Screenshot: Logs tab — raw event log table]

Pipeline

Shows the scanning agent data ingestion pipeline status. Each agent is listed with its last heartbeat, events processed in the last hour, and any pipeline errors. Green = healthy, amber = degraded, red = offline or stalled. Contact your admin if agents appear red.

[Screenshot: Pipeline tab — agent status table with colour-coded health indicators]

AI Inventory

The AI Inventory tab provides a per-user mind map of all AI assets PatronAI has discovered for each individual.

  1. At the top of the tab, a row of user pill buttons is shown — one pill per monitored user.
  2. Click a user's pill to select them. Their name pill highlights and the mind map renders below.
  3. The mind map shows the selected user at the centre, with branches for each AI provider they use. Sub-nodes show specific models, endpoints, and categories.
  4. Click any node in the mind map to drill into it — a side panel opens with details: request count, data categories involved, risk flags, and a timeline.
Note — Mind map rendering

The mind map may take a moment to render for users with a large number of AI assets. If the map appears blank, try selecting the user pill again.

[Screenshot: AI Inventory tab — user pills at top, mind map below with node selected and side panel open]

Support View support admin

The Support View adds eight operational and diagnostic tabs used by support engineers to manage rules, monitor agent health, and investigate coverage gaps.

[Screenshot: Support View — tab bar with all 8 tabs visible]

Support Tabs

Rules

Lists all active detection rules configured in PatronAI. Each rule shows: rule name, trigger condition, severity threshold, and enabled/disabled state. Contact your admin to add or modify rules (rule editing is in Settings → Scanning).

Code Signals

Displays behavioural signals extracted from code-related AI traffic — for example, code generation requests, repository names detected in context, and programming language distribution. Use this tab to understand how developers in your organisation interact with AI coding tools.

Coverage

Shows a coverage map — which users and devices are currently instrumented with scanning agents versus which are not. Uninstrumented endpoints appear in amber. Use this tab to identify coverage gaps and plan agent deployment.

Warning — Coverage gaps

Devices that appear amber in the Coverage tab are not being monitored. AI usage on those devices is invisible to PatronAI. Prioritise deploying agents to any amber devices that handle sensitive data.

Health

System health overview — shows the PatronAI backend services, scanning agent fleet aggregate statistics, S3 upload queue depth, and LLM service availability. Red indicators here require admin attention.

Logs

Same raw event log available in the Manager View, included here for support engineers investigating specific incidents without needing to switch to the Manager View.

Risks

Same filterable risks list as Manager View, available here for support engineers. Useful for working an incident ticket while staying in the Support View context.

Pipeline

Same pipeline status view as Manager View. Support engineers can monitor agent ingestion health directly from this tab.

Agent Fleet

Detailed view of every deployed scanning agent — hostname, IP, OS, agent version, last heartbeat, events sent today, and configuration version. Use this tab to identify agents that are out of date or have stopped reporting. Click an agent row to see its configuration details.

[Screenshot: Agent Fleet tab — table of agents with health status, version, and last heartbeat columns]

User Detail Page

The User Detail page is a deep-dive view for a single user. It is available to all roles that can see tables containing user emails.

How to access

Click any user's email address in any table across the dashboard — Inventory, drill-down panels, risk lists, or log tables. The User Detail page opens inline.

[Screenshot: User Detail page — risk summary at top, ASSETS tab selected with mind map, findings list below]

What the User Detail page shows

Risk Summary banner

At the top, a colour-coded banner shows the user's overall risk level (LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL), total findings count, active providers, and last-seen timestamp.

ASSETS tab

An interactive mind map of all AI assets tied to this specific user. This is the same mind map available in the Manager View AI Inventory tab, but pre-filtered to the selected user. Click any node to see detailed event data for that asset.

Findings

A chronological list of every finding associated with this user. Each finding shows: severity, category, timestamp, description, and the raw evidence snippet. Findings can be filtered by severity or date range directly on this page.

Tip — Back navigation

Use your browser's back button or the breadcrumb at the top of the page to return to the view you came from.

Reports (R1–R7) manager support admin

PatronAI includes seven pre-built report templates. Reports are generated on demand, previewed in the browser, and downloaded as PDF files. They include your company logo if one has been uploaded in Settings → Branding.

How to generate a report

  1. In the left sidebar, click Reports.
  2. Select the report type from the list (R1 through R7).
  3. Choose a date range using the start/end date pickers. For R3, also select a user from the dropdown.
  4. Click the 👁 Preview button. The report renders as formatted HTML inline in the page. Review it for accuracy.
  5. Click the ⬇ PDF button to download the finalized PDF to your computer.
Note — Logo in reports

Reports will display a generic PatronAI header until a company logo is uploaded. To add your logo, go to Settings → Branding.

[Screenshot: Reports page — date range pickers, Preview button, rendered HTML report preview, PDF download button]

Report Catalogue

R1

Executive Summary Report

High-level AI risk posture for leadership. Includes KPI summary, top risks, provider landscape, and trend direction over the selected period. One to two pages, optimised for board or leadership presentations.

R2

AI Findings Report

Full list of all AI findings within the date range. Grouped by severity (CRITICAL → LOW). Each finding includes timestamp, user, device, provider, category, and description. Suitable for security team review.

R3

User Risk Report

Per-user deep dive. Select a specific user from the dropdown. Report covers their risk score timeline, AI asset inventory, findings history, and behavioural patterns. Used for HR, compliance, or incident response.

R4

Provider Intelligence Report

Analysis of all AI providers detected in the environment. For each provider: usage volume, user count, data categories sent, first detected date, and risk classification. Useful for procurement and security policy decisions.

R5

Data Exposure Report

Focused on sensitive data categories detected in AI traffic — PII, financial, credentials, source code, and others. Shows volume, trend, and which users/providers are involved. Used for DLP and compliance reporting.

R6

Alert Activity Report

Summary of all alerts that fired during the period. Each alert shows: rule name, trigger time, affected user, severity, and whether it was acknowledged. Useful for SOC review and demonstrating control effectiveness.

R7

Coverage & Fleet Report

Agent deployment coverage report. Shows which users and devices are instrumented, which are not, agent version distribution, and pipeline health metrics. Used for audit evidence and coverage gap remediation tracking.

Ask AI — Chat Widget manager support admin

PatronAI includes a built-in AI chat assistant. Ask questions in plain English and receive answers derived directly from your organisation's security data. The chat is powered by a local LLM running on your EC2 instance — no data leaves your infrastructure.

Note — Privacy

The Ask AI feature uses Qwen 3.5 9B running locally on your EC2 instance via Ollama. Your organisation's data does not leave the server. No queries are sent to external AI services.

Opening the chat

  1. The chat widget appears as a collapsed bar at the bottom of the page in the Exec, Manager, and Support views.
  2. Click the widget bar (or the expand arrow) to open the chat panel.
  3. Type your question in the input field and press Enter to submit.
  4. Click the widget bar again to collapse it — your conversation is preserved.
Enter Submit message to AI
Shift + Enter Add a new line without submitting
[Screenshot: Ask AI widget — expanded, showing a conversation thread and input field at bottom]

Example questions

The AI can answer questions about your live data. Some examples:

"Who are my top 5 riskiest users?"
"Show me all CRITICAL findings this week."
"Which AI providers are new this month compared to last month?"
"Tell me about alice@company.com's AI footprint."
"How many devices are silent?"
"What data categories are being sent to ChatGPT?"
"Has there been any credential exposure in the last 30 days?"

Chat history

The conversation is saved between sessions. When you re-open the chat widget, your previous messages and responses are shown. To start a fresh conversation, click the Clear history button inside the chat panel.

Tip — Getting better answers

Be specific about time ranges and users when possible. "Show CRITICAL findings for alice@company.com this week" returns a much more targeted answer than "show me findings."

MCP Server admin only

The MCP (Model Context Protocol) Server allows Claude Desktop or Cursor IDE to query PatronAI directly from your development environment. Instead of switching to the browser dashboard, you can ask PatronAI questions directly inside your AI coding assistant.

Warning — SSH Access Required

The MCP server is secured by SSH key. Only users who have an SSH key authorised for your EC2 instance can connect. Do not share your SSH private key. Treat it with the same care as a password.

Setup

  1. After PatronAI is deployed, the admin deployment script prints a Claude Desktop configuration block to the terminal.
  2. Copy that configuration block.
  3. Open Claude Desktop. Go to Settings → Developer → Edit Config.
  4. Paste the configuration block into the JSON config file and save.
  5. Restart Claude Desktop. PatronAI will appear as a connected tool.

Example prompts in Claude Desktop

  • "What critical findings does PatronAI see right now?"
  • "Which users have accessed new AI providers in the last 7 days?"
  • "Get me the risk summary for bob@company.com."
  • "Are there any agents offline right now?"
  • "Show me the top 10 AI providers by usage volume this month."
Note — Who can use the MCP server

Only admin-flagged users configure and manage the MCP server. The MCP connection uses your SSH credentials, so each user who wants MCP access must have their own SSH key on the EC2 instance — contact your admin to arrange this.

Users Tab

Manage who has access to PatronAI — add users, remove users, and change roles.

Adding a user

  1. Click the Add User button.
  2. Enter the user's email address.
  3. Select their role from the dropdown: exec, manager, or support.
  4. Optionally check the Admin checkbox to grant admin flag access.
  5. Check the Send welcome email checkbox to email the user their login credentials automatically.
  6. Click Save. The user appears in the user list immediately.
Tip — Welcome email

Always check "Send welcome email" when creating a new user. It sends them their login credentials and a link to this user guide. If the email fails to send, a warning banner will appear — check that your SMTP settings are configured in the environment.

Removing a user

Find the user in the user table and click the Remove button on their row. Confirm the prompt. The user's account is deactivated immediately — they will not be able to log in. Their historical data is retained.

Changing a role

Click the role badge on any user's row in the table. A dropdown appears — select the new role. The change takes effect on the user's next page load.

[Screenshot: Settings → Users tab — user table with Add User form visible]

Branding Tab

Upload your organisation's logo. Once uploaded, it appears in the header of all generated PDF reports (R1–R7).

  1. Go to Settings → Branding.
  2. Click Upload Logo and select your logo file (PNG or SVG recommended, minimum 200 px wide).
  3. A preview of the logo is shown. If it looks correct, click Save.
  4. Generate any report to confirm the logo appears in the PDF output.
Note — Logo format

PNG with a transparent background works best. Avoid JPEG for logos as compression artefacts can appear in PDFs. Maximum file size is 2 MB.

[Screenshot: Settings → Branding tab — logo upload area and preview]

Deploy Agents Tab

Scanning agents are lightweight processes that run on endpoints (workstations, servers, VMs) and report AI usage data back to the PatronAI dashboard. This tab provides deployment instructions and agent status.

Deploying an agent to an endpoint

  1. Go to Settings → Deploy Agents.
  2. Select the target operating system (macOS, Windows, Linux).
  3. Copy the one-line install command shown on screen.
  4. Run that command on the target endpoint in a terminal with admin/sudo privileges.
  5. Within two minutes, the new agent should appear in the Support → Agent Fleet tab with a green heartbeat status.
Warning — Admin privileges required

Agent installation requires administrator (Windows) or sudo (macOS/Linux) privileges on the target endpoint. Agents that fail to install silently will not appear in Agent Fleet — confirm installation success before marking a device as covered.

[Screenshot: Settings → Deploy Agents tab — OS selector and install command display]

Other Settings Tabs

Scanning

Configure detection rules — create new rules, edit existing rule conditions, adjust severity thresholds, and enable/disable individual rules. Changes take effect within one scanning cycle (typically under one minute).

Alerting

Configure alert delivery channels — email recipients, alert thresholds, cooldown periods between repeat alerts for the same condition, and alert suppression windows (for example, during planned maintenance).

Identity

Configure how PatronAI maps network identities (IP addresses, device names) to user accounts. Useful when users share devices or when VPNs cause IP ambiguity.

Provider Lists

Manage the lists of known AI providers PatronAI tracks — add new providers, categorise them, mark them as approved or blocked in your policy, and adjust their risk weight.

Glossary

TermDefinition
AgentA lightweight process installed on an endpoint that monitors AI-related network traffic and sends findings to PatronAI.
CategoryA classification of AI usage purpose, e.g. Code Generation, Image Generation, Summarisation, Translation.
CoverageThe proportion of monitored users and devices that have active scanning agents installed.
Data ExposureDetection of sensitive data (PII, credentials, source code, etc.) present in traffic sent to an AI provider.
FindingA specific event that PatronAI has identified as noteworthy based on its detection rules — e.g. sensitive data sent to an unapproved provider.
HeartbeatA periodic signal sent by a scanning agent to confirm it is alive and connected. Missing heartbeats indicate an offline agent.
MCP ServerModel Context Protocol server — allows Claude Desktop and Cursor IDE to query PatronAI directly via SSH.
Mind MapAn interactive graph showing a user's AI assets — providers, models, categories — as connected nodes.
PipelineThe data ingestion path from scanning agent → PatronAI backend → dashboard. Pipeline health indicates whether data is flowing correctly.
ProviderAn AI service endpoint detected in network traffic, e.g. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Midjourney.
Risk ScoreA composite score (0–100) calculated for each user or device based on findings severity, volume, and data exposure.
Sankey DiagramA flow diagram where band widths are proportional to flow volume. Used in the AI Landscape tab to show traffic from users through categories to providers.
Silent DeviceA device that has an installed agent but has not sent any events in the expected window — potentially offline or misconfigured.

Frequently Asked Questions

I can't log in — what do I do?

Check that you are using the exact email address your admin registered. Passwords are case-sensitive. If you still cannot log in, contact your PatronAI admin — they can reset your credentials from Settings → Users.

I can see the dashboard but a tab is missing — why?

Your role determines which tabs are visible. Refer to the Roles & Access table. If you believe you need access to a tab not shown for your role, ask your admin to update your role in Settings → Users.

The Ask AI chat is not responding — what should I check?

The chat depends on the local LLM service (Ollama + Qwen 3.5 9B) running on the EC2 instance. If the chat shows a service error, contact your admin to check the LLM service health in Support → Health.

An agent shows as offline — is this a problem?

An offline agent means that endpoint is no longer reporting data. AI usage on that device is invisible until the agent is restored. Check Support → Agent Fleet for details and share the hostname with your admin for remediation.

How do I add my company logo to reports?

Admin users can upload a logo at Settings → Branding. Once saved, it appears in all subsequent PDF report downloads.

Does PatronAI send my data to external services?

No. PatronAI runs entirely on your EC2 instance. The AI chat LLM is local (Qwen 3.5 9B via Ollama). Your organisation's data does not leave your EC2 instance unless you explicitly export a report PDF.

Can I schedule reports to be sent automatically?

Scheduled report delivery is on the PatronAI roadmap. In the current version (v1.0.0), reports are generated on demand. You can generate and download PDFs from the Reports section at any time.
